Oyako-don

  • Description
  • Ingredients
  • Directions

Description

Oyako-don, literally "parent-and-child donburi", is a donburi, or Japanese rice bowl dish, in which chicken, egg, sliced scallion (or sometimes regular onions), and other ingredients are all simmered together in a sauce and then served on top of a large bowl of rice. The name of the dish is a poetic reflection of the fact that both chicken and egg are used in the dish. Wikipedia page

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken thighs
  • 3 Tbsp. mirin
  • 1 Tbsp. cooking sake
  • 1 cup dashi
  • 2 Tbsp. soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp. sugar
  • 1/2 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 large eggs, gently beaten
  • Finely sliced green onion for garnish
  • 3 cups cooked white rice

Directions

  1. Slice the chicken diagonally and cut into 1" (2.5 cm) pieces.
  2. In a large frying pan, add mirin and sake and bring to a boil over medium high heat.
  3. Add dashi, soy sauce and sugar, and bring to a boil again.
  4. Add the onion in a single layer and place the chicken on top. Cover and bring to a boil.
  5. Skim off the scum and fat, and cook over medium heat for about 10 minutes.
  6. Slowly and evenly drizzle the beaten egg over the chicken and onion. Cook covered until the egg is done. Shake the pan occasionally so the egg and chicken don't stick to the pan.
  7. Add the green onion right before removing from the heat. Pour the chicken and egg on top of steamed rice and pour desired amount of sauce.
